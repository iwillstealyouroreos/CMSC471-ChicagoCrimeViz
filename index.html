Chicago Crime Data Visualization

For this assignment, I used the Chicago crime dataset from the City of Chicago's data portal. This dataset contains detailed information about reported incidents of crime from 2001 to today. My focus was on the rise and changes in crime from 2018 to 2022 to understand how the pandemic may have impacted crime patterns.
I focused on four key variables:
  • The year the crime was reported (Year)
  • The type of crime (Type)
  • The district or location of the crime (District)
  • Whether an arrest was made (Arrest)
These variables were used in dynamic filters for the year and district, as well as in tooltips.
My goal was to explore how crime types evolved during the pandemic. I chose to use a TreeMap to visualize the magnitude of crime reported. I also removed crime entries below a reasonable threshold to avoid overcrowding and ensure meaningful insights. While I initially considered using geolocation data to create density or heatmaps, I eventually focused on TreeMap visualization, which aligned better with my objectives.
I also wanted to analyze crime data across different districts. This allows for comparison of crime patterns in various areas and better insights into local trends.
For the TreeMap implementation, I used the Chicago Crimes dataset along with techniques from d3-graph-gallery. I also utilized transitions and initialization functions provided during class and lab.
This project was completed individually, as most of my peers had already paired up. It was a challenging experience, but I gained valuable insights. The data cleaning process took a significant amount of time due to inconsistencies and missing data. Initially, I wanted to create spike maps using the geolocation data, but it was poorly documented. After selecting the TreeMap visualization, I followed a helpful tutorial and completed the implementation in 1-2 days.
Overall, the project was a great learning experience. I had a lot of fun, but I would definitely prefer working in a team for the next project to collaborate more effectively.
